About the Organization

A practical community for hardware and systems learning.

The Computer Hardware Club is a student org at OSU built around hands-on workshops and projects for people who love computer hardware and systems. We believe the best way to learn is by doing.

Mission

How we operate

We run workshops on real tools and topics, work on hands-on projects each term, and build a community of people who genuinely enjoy this stuff.

Applied learning

Members work with real hardware and systems problems rather than only theoretical exercises.

Inclusive technical culture

New members are supported from day one, and experienced members gain leadership opportunities.

Curiosity first

Expertise helps, but it's not required. A willingness to learn and tinker is what matters here.

Learning together

Workshops are collaborative, group experiences. You'll almost always be following along, asking questions, and building alongside your team or group.
